# Grammar & Style — oh.design

English is the default language. Swedish is a supported translation. The tone is professional but personal — like a confident designer talking to another designer.

We've got our own way of writing certain things, and we cover them here. It helps us to be consistent, whatever and wherever we're writing.

**Contents:** [Voice & Tone](#voice--tone) · [UI Copy](#ui-copy) · [Content patterns](#content-patterns) · [A–Z Reference](#az-reference) · [Swedish](#swedish-translation) · [Words to avoid](#words-to-avoid)

## Voice & Tone

- **Direct.** No filler words, no fluff. Say what needs to be said.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** State things clearly without over-explaining.
- **Human, not corporate.** Write like a person, not a press release.
- **Show, don't tell.** "Built with performance in mind" tells. A fast site shows.

---

## UI Copy

Short, scannable, action-oriented.

### Buttons & CTAs
- Use verbs: "View project", "Read more", "Get in touch"
- Never: "Click here", "Learn more" without context, "Submit"

### Labels & Navigation
- Sentence case, no punctuation at end
- Keep to 1–3 words where possible

### Empty states & placeholders
- Friendly, brief: "No projects yet" — not "There are currently no projects to display"

### Error messages
- Say what happened and what to do: "Couldn't send message — try again or email me directly"

---

## Content Patterns

### Project descriptions
1. One-line summary — what it is
2. The problem or context
3. What was done and why
4. Outcome (if relevant)

### Page headings
- Lead with the most important word
- Avoid starting with "A" or "The"

### A

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Acronyms** | Don't use an acronym unless it's commonly used, and spelling it out causes more confusion. | PDF | Portable document format |
| **Active voice** | Use active voice. The subject does the action — it's clearer and more direct. | The team designed the system | The system was designed by the team |
| **Ampersands** | Don't use &. Write "and" instead, unless the ampersand is part of a brand name. | Design and code | Design & code |

---

### B

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Brackets** | Only use brackets for abbreviations or data. Punctuation sits outside brackets if only part of the sentence is inside. | (like this) | (like this.) |
| **Bullet points** | One point per bullet. No full stops at the end. If the intro is a full sentence, bullets start with capitals. If not, no capitals. | — A design system | — a design system. |

---

### C

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Cases** | Sentence case everywhere — body copy, headings, buttons, links. Only proper nouns get capitals. | Featured work | Featured Work |
| **Change vs Edit** | Use "Change" when switching between options. Use "Edit" when modifying something that already exists. | Edit profile · Change theme | Change profile · Edit theme |
| **Choose vs Select** | Use "Choose" when any option could be true. Use "Select" when only one option can be true. | Choose a layout | Select your country |
| **Contractions** | Use them. They make writing feel human. | You're, can't, doesn't | You are, cannot, does not |

---

### D

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Dashes** | Em dash (—) with a space either side for interruptions or emphasis. En dash (–) for number ranges. | Great work — like this | Great work - like this |
| **Dates** | Day first, no ordinals. Include the day of the week only when it matters. | 1 March 2024 | 1st March 2024 |
| **Dropdown** | One word, no hyphen. | Dropdown | Drop-down |

---

### E

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **E.g.** | Write "for example" or "like" instead. | For example, like | E.g. |
| **Edit** | Use when modifying something existing. See Change vs Edit above. | Edit project | Change project |
| **Ellipses** | Use to show a thought that isn't finished. No spaces before or after. | I'm not sure...but I'll figure it out. | I'm not sure ... but I'll figure it out. |
| **Email** | No hyphen. | Email | E-mail |
| **Emoji** | Don't use them. | Words only | 🚀 |
| **Etc.** | Don't use it. Give examples instead. | — | Etc. |
| **Exclamation marks** | Rarely needed. One at a time, maximum. | Hey! | Hey there!!! |

---

### F

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Full stops** | Use in body copy. Not on headings or subheadings. If you need to breathe before the end of a sentence, add a full stop. | Heading\n\nAnd some body copy. | Heading.\n\nAnd some body copy |

---

### L

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Login** | Login (one word) is an adjective. Log in (two words) is a verb. | Login page · Log in to continue | Log in page · Login to continue |

---

### N

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Numbers** | Use numerals — they're easier to scan on screen. Spell out only when a number starts a sentence. | There are 9 projects. | There are nine projects. |

---

### O

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Oxford comma** | Use only when needed for clarity. | Two employees, an accountant, and a director | — |

---

### P

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Percentages** | Use the % symbol, not the word "percent". | 75% | 75 percent |
| **Perspective** | Write to the user in second person — "you", "your". Don't mix with first person on the same screen. | Your profile | My profile |

---

### S

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Select** | Use when only one option can be true, or when telling a user to tap/click something. More inclusive than "click". | Select a file | Click a file |
| **Slashes** | No spaces either side. | and/or | and / or |

---

### T

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Time** | Numerals with am/pm, space between. En dash for ranges. | 8:00 am · 7:00–11:30 pm | 8am · 7am-11:30pm |

---

### V

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **View** | Use "View" not "See" when directing users to look at something. | View project | See project |

---

### W

| Term | Rule | ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Wifi** | Lowercase, no hyphen. | wifi | Wi-Fi, WIFI |

## Swedish Translation

When translating to Swedish:

- Match the same direct, confident tone — avoid formal Swedish ("man bör") in favour of direct address ("du kan")
- Use sentence case for headlines, same as English
- Keep UI copy short — Swedish words are often longer, so tighten the copy
- "du" not "Du" (lowercase unless at sentence start)
- Dates: "1 mars 2024" (month lowercase in Swedish, no ordinals)

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| leverage | use |
| utilize | use |
| seamless | (just describe what it does) |
| intuitive | (show it, don't claim it) |
| world-class | (earned, never stated) |
| passionate | (same) |
| click here | descriptive CTA |
| etc. | give examples |
| e.g. | for example, like |
